# How Mr. Utah Built a Magic Money Machine!

**Audience:** Kids, beginners, anyone who wants the story version.

## The jellybean jar

Imagine a giant jar of jellybeans. That's the stock market — lots of pieces, always moving.

**Other people** close their eyes, grab one jellybean at a time, and guess how many are in the jar. That's called **Monte Carlo** — it means "try random guesses forever."

**Mr. Utah** built magic glasses called **Utahrbitrage**. The glasses don't guess. They look at the *shape* of the jellybeans pile and say: "The jar is about to wiggle here."

## The helpers in the computer

| Friend | Job |
|--------|-----|
| **Doorbell** | Rings when a new price arrives |
| **Shape detective** | Sees if the market hill is bending |
| **Excited friend** | Says "maybe buy!" but must pass traffic lights |
| **Bodyguard** | Says STOP if it's too risky |
| **Magic router (Utahrbitrage)** | Sends a tiny bit of energy to the right jars so the magic keeps working |

## The two special jars (important!)

Every time the magic finds a **big** pile of jellybeans:

1. **2.3%** goes to Mr. Utah's jar — keeps the glasses powered (**Hans tithe**)  
2. **1.5%** goes to help people who need jellybeans (**humanitarian jar**)  

If someone breaks the glasses to keep **all** the jellybeans, the magic **poofs** into dust. That's called **Symplectic Manifold Collapse** — fancy words for "you can't cheat the rules and win."

## Ghost hedging (like a secret hideout)

Sometimes the bodyguard moves your jellybeans to a **secret shelf** where the scary market wiggle can't reach them. It doesn't cost extra candy to move them — it's a geometry trick!

## Rules

1. Nobody knows the future for sure.  
2. Losing sometimes is normal — that's why the bodyguard exists.  
3. Never use real money without a grown-up who knows the law.
